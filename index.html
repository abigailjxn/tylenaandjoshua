GROOMSMEN

Jake Jeans

Best Man

There is no person in my life with whom I have fought more than Jake Jeans. We have each broken each others' bones and had mom threaten to call the police on us when we got too big for her to handle. That being said, I would die for him in a second and I know that he would do the same for me. We play hard and we love each other hard. I'm grateful that God gave me such a gift in a brother and I'm also grateful that God made me one inch taller.

Langston McElroy

Langston and I met when he and 13 other people piled into my old F150 (Big Mama, may she rest in peace) on our way to the state fair during my sophomore and his freshman year. Langston then went on to become my little in our fraternity and we met together every week for 4 years for discipleship, accountability, and encouragement. I've torn up many dance floors with this brother of mine. I've never met someone who can do so much damage with their singing voice and bring so much life when they speak- it's truly a gift.

Dean Poolos

Dean once tied me up and put me in the trunk of his 1999 Mercury Sable on senior skip day. It was weird but I felt safe the whole time. He and I became friends during the 8th grade when we had the same science class, but we grew closer during high school. We have been asked to leave restaurants MULTIPLE times for laughing too loud. It's hard for me to think of someone who brings so much joy and light-heartedness to my world as Dean Poolos!

Dan McNeil

Dan and I became friends during our sophomore year of college. We became good friends during our junior year, and subsequently became the best of friends our senior year when we voluntarily elected to share a small double-room in our on-campus apartment. I still smile like the Cheshire Cat whenever I get together with this guy and I admire his life and marriage deeply. Fun fact: I was the only one in the room when Dan found out he was accepted into medical school. In our excitement and because of our size difference, I lifted him into the air like baby Simba and we just screamed into each others' faces for probably thirty seconds.

Matt Girgis

Matt prayed for me at my first Navs Bible study during my freshmen year at Vanderbilt when I didn't know anyone and cried like a baby. We were soon after assigned to each other as "Pledge Buddies" as we sought membership in BYX. He has been a gracious friend and partner in crime upon many occasions. Matt has helped me grow and has loved me well. His most notable accomplishment in my life has probably been converting me from being a Moe's man to a Chipotle loyalist.

Turi Clausell

My tallest friend who truly gives me a run for my money on the dance floor, Turi (like Curry but with a "T") keeps me on a short leash and is truly a gift from God. We became good friends during my sophomore and his senior year at Vanderbilt. I've never met a person who thinks more similarly to me and I trolled Turi for years always pretending to forget what "ESP" meant (Extra Sensory perception- look it up) and instead insisting that he and I had ESV (the English Standard Version of the Bible). This was cringe-worthy at best for Turi as a Neuroscience and Psychology major, but for some reason he's kept me around!

Ted Moock

I still remember the day I thought to myself, "Ted and I are going to be friends for life." Like many white, semi-outdoorsy Christian fraternity brothers at an SEC school, it happened one night when we were chilling in our Enos (read: hammocks) and sharing about life- honestly you could call it pillow talk but I don't know how kosher that is. Ted is one of the most authentic people I have ever met, which is frankly very hard for someone as cool/popular as he is. He shares deeply and loves freely and is far more ratchet than you would anticipate. We lived together our junior year of college and he learned how to twerk from me and I almost learned how to harmonize from him. Almost.

Clay Myatt

Clay is probably my most sane friend. He is different from me in almost every way, but we have grow so close through college and beyond. Although he is much smarter than me, Clay and I studied the same thing at Vanderbilt and had the same part-time job on campus, so we spent a LOT of time together. I admire his faithfulness to the Lord and to those God has called him to love, and he has one of the better laughs you will ever hear. No seriously, make sure you hear him laugh at the wedding, it's all inhales, no exhales.

Why Nashville?

Great question! We’re glad that you asked. Nashville has played a big part in our relationship. It’s where we had our first kiss, our biggest fight, and it’s where we decided that we would take the risk on each other and become boyfriend and girlfriend while living 478 miles apart. We’ve been to concerts and parties here, cooked meals and gone on long walks, explored and rested. It is a city that we both love and it has loved us back.

And practically speaking, we did it for you guys! With our main friend and family bases being in Kansas, Chicago, Nashville, and Georgia, Nashville stood out as the most central option. Our hope was to help our friends and family get to our wedding! Nashville is within driving distance for more of our loved ones than any other option we looked into. Hopefully if you made I this far, you will make it to the 615 to celebrate with us! That’s right- Nashville’s area code and our wedding date are one-in-the-same!
